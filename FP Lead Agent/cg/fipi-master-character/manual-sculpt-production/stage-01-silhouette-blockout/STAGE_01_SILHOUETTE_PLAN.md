## Stage Boundary

This stage is not face sculpt, spine detail, retopo, UV, materials, rig, animation, or Figma insertion.

The Blender output, if present, is a temporary sculpt blockout candidate for silhouette review only. It is not production topology and must not be treated as an approved master.

## Proportion Decisions

- Overall read: compact, rounded, friendly plush mascot.
- Head/body: head and torso should feel like one soft continuous volume, not a sphere on a capsule.
- Upper head: wide and rounded, with crown spine mass increasing total height.
- Torso: broad rounded belly volume, slightly narrowing into short stable legs.
- Belly patch: soft oval/round patch on torso only; it must not rise into the face.
- Arms: short, soft, hanging/raised-friendly proportion; hands land around lower belly/upper hip height.
- Legs/feet: short, wide, stable stance; feet are rounded and toe masses are only roughly indicated in this stage.
- Ears: large side ears, round, positioned around eye/head band height.
- Face placement only: rough eye/nose/mouth anchors are included to check scale and identity zones, but no detail pass is attempted.

## Front Silhouette Targets

- Blue crown and side spine shell frames the green head.
- Green face remains fully green.
- Eye band sits in upper-middle head zone.
- Nose sits centered below eye band.
- Smile anchor remains under nose, but only as rough placement.
- Belly patch occupies torso, centered, with top edge below face/head transition.
- Arms sit outside torso silhouette without becoming long human arms.
- Feet create a stable base without increasing total height too much.

## Side Silhouette Targets

- Nose projects gently forward, not sharply.
- Torso belly projects forward as soft plush volume.
- Blue spine shell sits behind head/back as a large continuous volume.
- Legs remain short and stable.
- Arm hangs as a short rounded mass near side torso.
- Ear sits between front face mass and rear spine shell.

## Back Silhouette Targets

- Back is dominated by a blue spine volume shell.
- Spine mass covers crown, back, and side read, but this stage keeps it as broad massing.
- Green body and limbs remain visible at sides/lower back.
- Legs and feet remain short, wide, and grounded.
- No detailed plastic leaf spines are attempted in Stage 1.

## Reference Mapping

- Turnaround front: drives broad head, ear size, torso-only belly patch, short limbs, stable feet.
- Turnaround side: drives forward face/nose projection, belly projection, rear spine-shell depth.
- Turnaround back: drives full blue back-shell footprint and rounded lower spine silhouette.
- Proportion guide: confirms eye band, torso patch boundary, spine shell mass, and compact overall height.
- Expression sheet: used only for rough face placement proportions, not expression/detail.
- Material closeups: used only to remember plush/soft form language; no material/lookdev pass is performed.

## Stage 1 Risks

- Scripted temporary blockout can check silhouette but cannot replace manual sculpt judgment.
- Face identity cannot be accepted from this stage because facial forms are only placeholders.
- Spine shell position can be reviewed, but detailed strand pattern must wait for later manual sculpt pass.
- Feet/toes and hands/fingers are only massed; exact sculpt readability must be checked in Stage 4.
- The side/back references are approved candidates, but still generated references; FP Lead should confirm any back spine layout concerns before detail sculpt.

## Stop-Loss Notes

If the blockout does not read as Fipi from front, side, and back without detailed face/spines, do not continue to detail. Return to silhouette sculpt revision.

## Stage Review Criteria

- Front, side, and back read as the same approved Fipi reference.
- Belly patch remains torso-only.
- Spine mass is positioned as a volume shell, not a detailed plastic-leaf pass.
- Limbs, ears, feet are proportionally close.
- No face or spine detail is used to hide a silhouette mismatch.

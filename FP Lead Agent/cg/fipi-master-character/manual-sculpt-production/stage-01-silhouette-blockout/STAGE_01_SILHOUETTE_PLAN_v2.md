## v2 Revision Decisions

- Replaced the v1 single smooth blue shell with layered rounded spine masses arranged in crown, side, and back rows.
- Kept spine forms broad and soft so they read as silhouette/massing, not final detailed plastic leaves.
- Increased hand/foot readability with palm, finger, foot base, and toe mass markers while avoiding sculpt-detail approval claims.
- Adjusted the green body into overlapping rounded head, lower-face/torso bridge, and torso masses to reduce the pure ball/capsule read.
- Preserved torso-only belly patch.
- Kept face elements as rough placement markers only.

## Front Silhouette Notes

Target from approved turnaround:

- wide friendly green head framed by blue crown and side spine masses;
- large side ears around the eye/head band;
- compact rounded torso with belly patch below face zone;
- short arms and small readable hands near lower belly/hip height;
- short legs and wide feet with toe massing;
- no light belly color on face.

v2 result:

- closer than v1 in spine crown/side row read and hand/foot readability;
- still not close enough in facial-region placement and head/body continuity;
- head still reads as constructed blockout mass rather than manual plush sculpt;
- front should remain under revision before any face/detail stage.

## Side Silhouette Notes

Target from approved turnaround:

- rounded face and belly project forward;
- blue spines layer backward along head/back;
- arm hangs short along side torso;
- feet are stable and short.

v2 result:

- side/back spine volume now reads as layered rows/masses instead of one smooth shell;
- side massing is closer to the reference but still needs manual sculpt refinement for natural plush flow;
- head/body transition remains too segmented.

## Back Silhouette Notes

Target from approved turnaround:

- back is dominated by dense blue spine rows;
- green limbs and lower body remain visible;
- lower spine silhouette rounds into the body, not a flat panel.

v2 result:

- rows are now visible as broad soft masses;
- silhouette is closer than v1 smooth shell;
- individual spine masses are still too mechanical/repeated for final approval, but acceptable as a Stage 1 revision study only.

## Remaining Risks

- Scripted temporary blockout cannot replace a true manual sculpt for Fipi's organic plush silhouette.
- Face anchors are placeholders and must not be reviewed as face identity.
- The head/body transition and spine rows still need manual sculpt judgment.
- Back spine row pattern is not final and must be confirmed before later detail sculpt.

## Stop-Loss
